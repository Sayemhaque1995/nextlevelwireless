Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions? We've got answers to some common inquiries below. Feel free to contact us if you don't see your question here.

How long do repairs usually take?

Many common repairs, like iPhone screen or battery replacements, can often be done in under an hour, sometimes while you wait! More complex repairs like micro-soldering or computer issues may take longer. We'll give you an estimated timeframe when you bring your device in.

Do I need an appointment?

Appointments are recommended, especially for more involved repairs, to ensure the quickest service. You can book online via our contact form or call us. However, walk-ins are always welcome, and we'll do our best to accommodate you as quickly as possible.

What is your warranty policy?

We proudly offer a 90-day warranty on all our repairs, covering both the parts we install and the labor performed. This warranty covers defects in the parts or workmanship but does not cover accidental damage (like drops or liquid) after the repair.

Do you use original parts?

We use high-quality aftermarket parts that meet or exceed original manufacturer (OEM) specifications. These parts offer excellent performance and reliability at a more affordable price point. We source our parts carefully and stand behind them with our warranty.

Can you fix water-damaged devices?

Yes, we offer water damage treatment. Success depends heavily on the type of liquid, how long the device was exposed, and how quickly you bring it in. We perform a specialized cleaning and diagnostic process. While we have a good success rate, water damage repair outcomes can vary, and we cannot guarantee data recovery in all cases. Don't try to turn it on or charge it - bring it to us ASAP!

Do you buy used phones or devices?

No, currently Next Level Wireless focuses solely on repairs, accessories, and activations. We do not purchase used devices from customers at this time.

What if you can't fix my device?

We offer free diagnostics. If we determine that your device cannot be repaired or that the cost of repair would be prohibitive, we will let you know upfront. For many logic board repairs, we operate on a "No Fix, No Fee" basis (diagnostic fees may apply in some complex cases, which we'll discuss beforehand).
